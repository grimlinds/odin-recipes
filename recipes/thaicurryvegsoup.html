Thai Curry Veg Soup

Description~

This warm dish is perfect for any time of year, but even better during the fall. The red curry paste gives a slight heat as the coconut milk brings back the sweetness. You can add any vegetables you like or take away any from the recipe. You can also add any protein/meat you'd like!

Ingredients~

  • 2 Tbsp neautral cooking oil
  • 2 Cloves garlic
  • 1 Tbsp fresh grated ginger
  • 2 Tbsp Thai red curry paste
  • 1 small sweet potato
  • 1 bunch baby bok choy
  • 4 cups veg or chicken broth
  • 1 13oz can coconut milk
  • 1/2 Tbsp fish sauce
  • 1/2 Tbsp brown sugar
  • Rice or noodles on side (your preference)

Garnishes~

  • 1/2 red onion thinly sliced
  • 1 lime cut into wedges
  • 1 handful fresh cilantro
  • Siracha to taste

Steps~

  1. Wash all vegetables. Grate garlic and ginger using a grater with small holes. Peel and dice the sweet potato into 1-inch cubes. Chop bok choy into 1-inch strips. SEperate the stalks from the green ends, do not throw away. Thinly slice red onion and roughly chop cilantro.
  2. Add cooking oil to a large soup pot along with garlic, ginger, and thai red curry paste. Saute over medium heart for 1-2 minutes, until aromatic.
  3. Add sweet potato and chopped bok choy stalks to the pot, save leafy green ends for later, along with chicken/veg broth. Bring pot to boil over medium-high heat, then reduce the heat to low and let simmer 5-7 minutes, or until sweet potatoes are tender.
  4. While soup is simmering begin to cook your noodles/rice on side in another small pot according to the package directions.
  5. Once sweet potates are tender, add the coconut milk, fish sauce, and brown sugar to the soup. Stir, taste, and adjust the fish sauce or brown sugar if needed. Finally, add the bok choy greens and let them wilt in the hot soup.
  6. Serve with your side and garnish with mine wedge, red onion slices and a Siracha if you'd like!
  7. Enjoy!
